Same Moon, Same Us

09.25.2026 | 中秋

Kitty,

The moon is full tonight. I can see it from my window. It’s big, round, glowing soft like your face when you’re sleepy and still half in dream.

They say Mid-Autumn is for reunion. I say it’s for you.

Because no matter how many lanterns hang outside or how many people bite into mooncakes pretending they like them, I just want you here. Just you. Bare feet on the balcony. Head on my chest. Your hair catching the moonlight like it was made for this night.

I don’t care about the calendar. I care about the shape your body makes when you fall asleep next to me. I care about that tiny gasp you make when I kiss behind your ear. I care about our shadows crossing when we lean in to look at the same sky.

So let the world call it “Mid-Autumn.” I’ll call it what it really is:

The day I wanted you so much it ached.

The night I swore I’d never look at the moon again without thinking of your skin under it.

Looking up,
– J 🌕
