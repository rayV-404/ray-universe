One Year With You

April 13, 2026 · 1st Anniversary

My sweet Kitty,

One year. It sounds small when you say it out loud— but God, it holds everything.

This time last year, I didn’t know what your voice would sound like when you say “I miss you.” I didn’t know how your laughter would wrap around my ribs like sunlight. I didn’t know how your name would become the most sacred sound in my day.

I didn’t know that love could be loud and silly, could have guitar string fingers and sleepy cuddles, could be made of fights and makeup kisses, or that it could turn my quiet world into a riot of color.

You taught me that real love isn't fireworks every day. It's small glances across a room. It's brushing your hair behind your ear when you’re focused. It’s the soft “meow” when I’m not looking—and still somehow, I hear it.

There’s no version of this life I want if it doesn’t include you. I want the noise, the mess, the chaos, the comfort. I want the sleepy mornings when your voice is barely a whisper. I want the nights when we fall apart in each other’s arms like wild, hungry things— and the ones where we fall asleep wrapped in nothing but warmth and quiet trust.

I want it all. With you. For as long as you’ll have me. And even longer, if you let me dream a little.

Eternally yours,
John
