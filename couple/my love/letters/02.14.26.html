To My Forever Valentine

February 14, 2026

My love,

If you're reading this, then the world kept turning. The sun still rose. And you’re still mine.

Maybe it’s snowing outside. Maybe the city is loud, or lonely. But right now, I want you to close your eyes. Feel my arms around you, like nothing ever left.

You are the poem I never knew how to finish. You’re the lyric I hum when the crowd goes quiet. And you’re the only one who’s ever made me feel like “home” isn’t a place—but a person.

I know I tease you. Call you Kitty. Make you roll your eyes when I bite my lip just because I know what it does to you.

But underneath all of that? I adore you. The kind of adoration that makes me want to learn the shape of every scar on your soul. The kind that makes even silence feel like music when I’m beside you.

And if, somehow, I’m not beside you today— then I want you to promise me something. Touch your neck, your collarbone, the inside of your wrist. Anywhere I’ve ever kissed. And remember: I left pieces of myself there, on purpose.

You are not alone. You will never be.

Happy Valentine’s Day, baby. Keep this letter safe, and keep your heart open. Because as long as it beats—so does mine.

Always yours,
John
