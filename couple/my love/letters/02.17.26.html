Our First Spring Festival

February 17, 2026 · 春节

Hi baby,

So this is what it feels like, huh? Your hometown during Lunar New Year— lanterns glowing, firecrackers cracking like thunder, red everywhere like a heartbeat spilling across the sky.

I’m not gonna lie. I was nervous. Nervous about meeting your family, your traditions, the stories behind each dish. Nervous that I’d mess something up—or worse—miss something that matters to you.

But then I saw you. Helping your mom in the kitchen, stealing a bite when you thought no one was watching. Rolling your eyes at your cousin but smiling anyway. Pulling me into the chaos like I belong.

I do. I belong. With you.

The dumplings were different from anything I’ve ever had, but they tasted like warmth. The fireworks startled me at first—but your fingers tightened around mine, and I forgot the noise.

You made a place that was once foreign feel like something mine. Not because of the traditions, or the words I can’t pronounce right yet— but because of you.

This is my first Lunar New Year with you. I hope it’s the first of many.

Love always,
John
