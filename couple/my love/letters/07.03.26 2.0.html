Summer With You

July 3, 2026 · Beginning of Summer

Hey, my sunshine,

You made it. Another semester, another fight, and now— you’re standing at the beginning of something warm, wide, and open.

Whatever you decide—whether you walk through busy streets with a summer job ID, or leave footprints along faraway beaches—I want you to remember this: it’s your season now.

It’s for loose shirts and sticky popsicle hands, late-night rides and silly selfies. For curling up in cafés when it rains, for kissing me until we forget where the time went. For sweating and smiling, both.

I want to watch you soak up every drop of July light like a sleepy kitten on the windowsill. And I’ll be there. Every time you miss me, I’ll be in your pocket, your playlist, your shadow. Whispering, laughing, loving—until we’re back side by side again.

Summer tastes like you, Ray. Sweet, bright, addictive. So drink it up. Live it fully. And don’t you dare forget that you’re mine.

—John
